- EVENTS AND FESTIVALS IN SRI LANKA -

Sri Lanka is a country that is rich in culture and heritage and there are numerous festivals and events that take place throughout the year. From religious events to cultural celebrations, there is always something to watch and experience in this lovely country. In this article, we will explore some of the most popular festivals and events in Sri Lanka and the information you need to experience them firsthand.

Religious Festivals

Vesak

Sri Lanka is predominantly a Buddhist country, and several Buddhist festivals are celebrated with great fervour. The most notable of them is Vesak Poya Day. Vesak is celebrated in May and commemorates the birth, enlightenment, and death of Lord Buddha. During this festival, the streets of the island are illuminated with colourful lanterns, and temples and homes are decorated with lights and oil lamps. In addition to these visual treats travellers can enjoy "Dansal" which is a custom of giving out food in stalls in this time period.

Dates you can visit - Full moon Poya day in May
Place you can visit - Throughout the country

Esala Perahara

The Kandy Esala Perahera or the Dalada Perahara is celebrated in July or August annually and is one of the most significant cultural events in Sri Lanka. The festival is held in the city of Kandy and features a grand procession with traditional dances, decorated elephants, fire dancers and musicians. The event is meant to honour the Sacred Tooth Relic of the Buddha, preserved in the Temple of the Tooth in Kandy. The Esala Perahera started in the 3rd century BC and is a spectacular event that attracts thousands of visitors from around the world.

Dates you can visit - Full moon Poya day in July
Place you can visit - Kandy

Duruthu Perahera

The Duruthu Perahera is celebrated in January and commemorates the Buddha's first visit to Sri Lanka. This is a significant Buddhist festival and is celebrated on the day of the full moon as well. The festival celebrates the first visit of Buddha to the island. Similar to the Esala Perahara, this celebration features local dances, whip crackers, fire dancers and elephants. This display of Sri Lanka's rich culture and spirituality attracts thousands and is a must-see event for tourists.

Dates you can visit - Full moon Poya day in January
Place you can visit - Kelaniya

Deepavali Festival

Deepavali, also known as the Festival of Lights, is a significant Hindu festival celebrated in Sri Lanka. The festival marks the triumph of good over evil and light over darkness. During the festival, people light oil lamps, decorate their homes with colourful lights, and offer prayers to the goddess Lakshmi, the goddess of wealth. It is also a time for feasting delicious sweets and exchanging gifts. Visitors to Sri Lanka can experience the vibrant and joyous atmosphere of Deepavali, a beautiful celebration of faith, culture, and community.

Dates you can visit - October or Novemeber each year
Place you can visit - Hindu Kovil (Temple)

Nallur Festival

Another stunning attraction is the Hindu Nallur Festival, celebrated annually in Jaffna, Sri Lanka. This festival dedicated to Lord Murugan lasts for 25 days during the Tamil month of Aadi, featuring decorated chariots, traditional music, dance performances, and fire-walking ceremonies. The highlight of the festival is the final day grand procession, the return of the deity to the Kovil. This Festival is a unique cultural experience making it a must-see for visitors.

Dates you can visit - August each year
Place you can visit - Jaffna

Cultural and National Festivals

Sinhala and Tamil New Year

The Sinhala and Tamil New Year, also known as the Aluth Avurudhha , is one of the most significant cultural festivals in Sri Lanka. Celebrated in mid-April, the festival marks the end of the harvest season and the beginning of the new year. The festival features a range of traditional activities, including lighting oil lamps, preparing special sweets, playing games, and exchanging gifts. One thing travellers can look out for is the song of the "Koha" bird which can only be heard in this period. It's a time for families to come together and celebrate, and visitors to the island can experience the unique customs and traditions of this colourful and joyous festival.

Dates you can visit - in April each year
Place you can visit - Throughtout the country

Independance Day

Sri Lanka Independence Day is celebrated annually on February 4th, to commemorate the country's independence from British rule in 1948. The day is marked by parades, cultural shows, flag-hoisting ceremonies etc. The main celebration takes place in Colombo, where the President raises the national flag and delivers a speech. Visitors to Sri Lanka can witness the patriotism and national pride of the people on this historic day, which is an essential part of the country's cultural heritage.

Dates you can visit - 4th of February each year
Place you can visit - Independence Square in Colombo


Sri Lanka is a country that celebrates its rich heritage and diverse culture through a variety of festivals and events throughout the year. From religious festivals to cultural events and national holidays, there is always something to celebrate in Sri Lanka. So, plan your visit accordingly and witness the vibrant and colourful festivals of this country.
